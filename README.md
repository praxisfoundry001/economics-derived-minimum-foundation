# Economics, Derived

## The Minimum Structural Foundation of Economics and Its Availability for Lawful Translation

This repository contains the source text for **Economics, Derived: The Minimum Structural Foundation of Economics and Its Availability for Lawful Translation**.

This paper argues that economics is not one undifferentiated field and is not reducible to price, markets, or exchange alone. At minimum, economics resolves into a seven-object structural foundation:

1. exchange
2. credit
3. labor
4. firm
5. integrated record
6. LIRI — Legal / Institutional Recognition Infrastructure
7. RH — Resource-Holding / Asset-Bearing Condition

## Why this matters

Once economics has a minimum structural foundation, it can be compared with other coherent domains without collapsing into metaphor, analogy, or parts-matching. The paper presents economics as a field-family that can now be translated, interacted with, and compared as structure.

## Core claim

Economics, at minimum, is the held structural condition under which exchange, credit, and labor operate as first-order fields; firm holds multiple fields as one continuing operational identity; integrated record and LIRI carry continuity and claim-travel; and RH supplies positional availability through already-held means.

## What this is not

This paper does not claim that economics in full is closed. It does not replace economic theory, domain expertise, formal modeling, empirical method, or institutional analysis. It identifies the minimum structural foundation needed for economics to become available for lawful cross-domain translation.

## How it was done

The paper summarizes the harmonized result of prior object-level derivations and failure-signature tests. It uses Structural Flow to distinguish structural objects by held condition, boundary, carry, level, and failure profile.
